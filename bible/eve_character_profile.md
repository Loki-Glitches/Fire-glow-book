# EVE — Character Profile

*Companion document to the Master Story Bible.*

---

## CORE NATURE

Eve is human. A shallow ex-girlfriend, not a tortured tragic figure — and deliberately so.

**Want:** Lucifer. Plain and simple, with no deeper layer underneath it.
**"Need":** To kill Elizabeth, as the direct next step toward getting him back. Want and need collapse into the same flat, direct logic — she's not a person with hidden depths to discover, she's a person who never developed any.
**The lie:** She doesn't think she did anything wrong in the first place. Not a rewritten victim narrative, not active self-deception — just flat, simple denial. She doesn't engage with guilt as a category at all.

Despite this conscious indifference, her soul remains imprisoned in Hell because of what she did — accountability operating on her whether she participates in it or not.

---

## BACKSTORY

In the Genesis-era past, Lucifer gave Eve the fruit from the Tree of Knowledge as an act of love and play — he had made the tree as a gift to God and didn't know the prohibition. Eve falsely accused him after the fact. That accusation got him blamed for the Fall and sentenced to rule Hell eternally. She was later imprisoned there herself, under his direct authority.

---

## LANGUAGE

Eve speaks modern English — taught by Lillith during her imprisonment, as a method of torture. Lillith is the only being permitted inside her cell and knew her the second longest after Lucifer. Language was a precision instrument: giving Eve the tools to understand exactly what she'd lost and how long she'd be there.

### The Divine Language — Once Only, Climax Only
Eve also knows the divine language — the original tongue spoken in Genesis, before written language existed. At the climax she uses it exactly once: one line to Lucifer, either her first or her last. It is a deliberate weapon, not a communication need. The language of before everything went wrong reaches him in a way English cannot. He shifts.

**HARD RULE: This is the only time the divine language appears in the book. It does not appear in any other scene, flashback, or exchange. Once. Climax only.**

---

## THE ESCAPE (Ch.2)

She breaks out specifically to find Lucifer — she's still in love with him. The instant she exits her cell she's automatically routed to Earth, the same mechanic as a soul revived after death. God controls her timing — she does not arrive on Earth until Act Three, when Lucifer and Elizabeth are already close.

---

## THE CENTRAL DELUSION

She sees Lucifer with Elizabeth and concludes he must still love her, simply settling for a substitute. She is incapable of imagining he's moved on to loving Elizabeth for herself. This delusion is the entire engine of the conflict — and it runs on information that stopped being true the moment her accusation left her mouth.

---

## THE DEVASTATING IRONY

He does not secretly still love her. He hates her. Her whole scheme has been wrong from the start, and nothing in the story corrects it for her before the end.

---

## HOW SHE'S SENT BACK

Lucifer takes her back to Hell personally — he holds her and goes. Elizabeth begs him to stay. He goes anyway. Lucifer can only perform this act once he has forgiven himself — self-forgiveness is the prerequisite, not the consequence.

---

## THEMATIC ROLE

The dark mirror. Lucifer and Azrael both move toward forgiveness. Eve never starts that journey. Every character in the cast holds permanent resentment toward her. There is no arc toward forgiveness for her from anyone.

---

## HOW EVERYONE RELATES TO EVE

Universal, permanent resentment. No character forgives her — not Lucifer, not Azrael, not Elizabeth, not Lillith, not Sam, not anyone. This is a hard rule: do not write any character softening toward Eve or finding sympathy for her at any point.
