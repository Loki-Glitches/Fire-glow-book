# SAM — Character Profile

*Companion document to the Master Story Bible.*

---

## WANT / NEED / LIE

**Want:** For Elizabeth to get everything she wants out of life.
**Need:** To get his own life back on track — something he's been quietly deferring since before it was ever really his to have.
**The lie:** *Lucifer is not good for Liz.* His distrust is genuine, straightforward protective-older-brother instinct — he simply doesn't know Lucifer. It just so happens, as a side effect rather than by design, that staying focused on protecting her keeps him from ever turning toward his own life.

---

## BACKSTORY

Their parents won the lottery and became chronically absent — funding the essentials (house, food, college) while disappearing for months at a time to travel. Both Sam and Elizabeth carry an abandonment wound from this. The parents left before Sam was 18, which means he gave up enjoyment and being a kid — not one dramatic sacrifice, just the whole texture of a normal adolescence quietly handed over without being asked. Sam has been the caretaker ever since.

**Sam knows Elizabeth is hiding something** but has nothing concrete to go on and is too stretched thin to pursue it. He catches the edge of it and puts it down. Neither of them has the bandwidth to hold the other's truth right now — a quiet mutual protection that keeps them both more isolated than either intends.

---

## ARC: RESOLVES — OFF-PAGE

While Lucifer is with Elizabeth, Tristan seeks Sam out to get to know Elizabeth through the person who knows her best — part of his formal evaluation process. Sam and Sophie end up convincing Tristan to accept the relationship. Not through a speech or a grand gesture — just two people who love Elizabeth being honest about what they've witnessed: it makes both of them better.

Sam doesn't need to go looking for a moment to give his blessing. Tristan comes to him. The lie drops quietly, through the side door, exactly the way Sam would let it.

**How the reader knows it happened:** Tristan's position shifts noticeably toward yes in the next Full Bond evaluation scene. No explicit callback needed — the reader connects it.

At the departure Sam watches Lucifer leave. He already gave his blessing, off-page, without ceremony. That's very Sam.
