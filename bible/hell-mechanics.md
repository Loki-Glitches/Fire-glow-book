# HELL — Mechanics Reference

*Companion document to the Master Story Bible. Writers read this before drafting any Hell-set scene.*

---

## PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION

Hell is a vast, dark, suffocating space. Cold. The only light comes from the glow leaking through cell doors — the suffering inside the cells is the only illumination in the entire realm. Dark hallways stretch as far as anyone can see, lined with cells on every side.

At the center of Hell stands a mountain of brimstone. Eve's cell is directly beneath it — she was among the first to arrive and the oldest souls are closest to the mountain. The further from the mountain, the more recently the soul died. It works on a first-come-first-serve proximity basis.

Lucifer's fortress sits at the peak of the mountain. It contains:
- A throne room with the Tree of Knowledge at its center
- Long hallways lined with paintings from different centuries
- A spiral staircase
- A cavern system beneath

## HOW SOULS ARRIVE

When a person dies, the soul naturally wants to ascend — it is weightless by default and moves upward toward Heaven. Guilt is a literal physical weight. However much guilt a soul carries, that weight pulls it downward toward Hell. No judgment, no trial, no divine decree — the soul simply goes where its weight takes it.

Once in Hell, the soul tries to break its chains. It cannot.

### The Psychopath Rule
Some people on Earth have a disconnected nerve in the brain that prevents emotion from being processed. They feel no guilt while alive — not because their soul lacks it, but because the body's wiring cannot carry the signal. When they die, the soul is separated from the faulty body. For the first time, the guilt lands — fully, all at once, with no prior experience of emotion to cushion it. They arrive in Hell feeling everything for the first time simultaneously. This is arguably the worst possible arrival condition.

## THE FORGIVENESS SYSTEM — WHY IT'S BROKEN

The demons torture souls by forcing them to relive their guilt over and over. This is the wrong job. Reliving the wound does not resolve it — it just reopens it. The system keeps producing pain without producing movement toward absolution.

Lucifer knows emotion is the missing mechanism. He has the material — he understands what needs to happen. He cannot implement it because he cannot access emotion himself yet. This is addressed in Book Two. Hell's forgiveness system does not improve in this book.

## HIERARCHY

**Administrative/Authority chain:**
Lucifer → Lillith → Demons (labor, seniority)
Lucifer → Sebastian → Human Patrol (administration, reporting)

Lillith sits above both structures. Demons outrank Sebastian and the human patrollers by nature — they are older and do more work — but Sebastian reports directly to Lucifer rather than through Lillith.

### Lillith
The first demon. Only being permitted inside Eve's cell. Full regent authority in Lucifer's absence. See Lillith character profile.

### Demons
Always in cells, always torturing — reliving guilt with the souls they're assigned to. They do not leave their function. They are the oldest beings in Hell after Lillith and carry seniority over the human patrollers.

### Sebastian
Lucifer's first human patroller and closest friend in Hell. Receives reports from the human patrol and brings discrepancies directly to Lucifer. The two talk when Sebastian has nothing urgent to report — genuine companionship, not just professional contact.

**Why Sebastian is here:** Lucifer handpicks human souls for the patrol role when they carry complicated guilt that the system cannot resolve — souls that need to feel like they're doing something good while their absolution remains unsolved. Sebastian was the first. His specific guilt is not documented here.

**Sebastian and Elizabeth:** Lucifer tells Elizabeth about Sebastian and Hell's structure. She never visits Hell directly. Sebastian never appears on Earth.

### Human Patrollers
Souls selected by Lucifer for the same reason as Sebastian — complicated, unresolvable guilt given productive purpose. They patrol Hell's corridors watching for discrepancies (crumbling brimstone, weakening cells, unusual activity) and report to Sebastian.

---

## THE TREE OF KNOWLEDGE

Sent to Hell with Lucifer when he was cast in. Sits at the center of his throne room. Its presence there is not incidental — it is the origin of everything, kept where only he has to look at it.

---

## WHAT HELL IS NOT

- Not Dante's levels — no tiered geography, no organized circles
- Not dramatically fiery everywhere — cold, dark, and suffocating is the register
- Not a place of grand theatrical punishment — it is vast, dim, and endless
