# SEBASTIAN — Character Profile

*Companion document to the Master Story Bible.*

---

## ROLE

Hell's chief patroller and Lucifer's closest companion in Hell. He receives reports from the human patrol, identifies discrepancies, and brings them directly to Lucifer. He was the first human soul Lucifer ever selected for this role — chosen when Lucifer noticed the brimstone crumbling from the strength of certain prisoners and needed eyes on the ground.

---

## RELATIONSHIP WITH LUCIFER

Loyal — he does whatever Lucifer says without question. But the relationship has grown beyond professional. When Sebastian has no news to deliver, the two talk. Genuine companionship in a place that has very little of it. Lucifer cannot lie and is surrounded by demons and suffering souls; Sebastian is the one person in Hell he actually speaks to as a person.

There is a quiet weight underneath this: Lucifer genuinely likes Sebastian and cannot help him. Sebastian's guilt is complicated enough that the system cannot resolve it — that's why he's here doing this job rather than in a cell. The absolution problem sits unanswered between them, unspoken.

---

## WHY HE'S HERE

Lucifer selects human souls for the patrol role when their guilt is too complicated for standard containment to resolve — souls that need to feel like they're doing something good while their absolution remains unsolved. Sebastian was the first. His specific guilt in life is not documented — it is not relevant to this book.

---

## IN THIS BOOK

Sebastian does not appear on Earth. Elizabeth learns about him and Hell's structure through Lucifer telling her directly. Sebastian's role is to make Hell feel like a real, administered place with texture and relationships — not just a backdrop.

---

## PERSONALITY

Capable, direct, slightly exasperated with Lucifer in the way someone gets with a boss they genuinely respect but who occasionally makes their job harder. Loyal without being sycophantic. He pushes back when he has something to say and defers when the decision is made.
